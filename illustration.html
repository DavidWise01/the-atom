The Atom, Drawnr 0–2 nucleus · r 3–10 electron field

a quarter-atom on a 10×10 grid — every particle (every “-on”) labeled
Honest scale note. This grid is a SCHEMATIC, not to scale. On the grid the nucleus is 2/10 of the radius; in reality it's about 1/100,000 — if this electron field filled the grid, the true nucleus would be far smaller than a single pixel. The point of the picture is the two zones and their parts, not their sizes: a strong-force nucleus (r 0–2) of protons & neutrons made of quarks bound by gluons, and an electromagnetic electron field (r 3–10) of shells, electrons, and the photons they trade — a million-to-one apart in energy.
