✦ Things you might not know

Is gravity part of the atom? Technically yes — every particle has mass, so gravity is present. But between two protons it's about 10³⁶ times weaker than their electromagnetic repulsion: utterly negligible inside an atom. Gravity builds stars and galaxies; it does essentially nothing to chemistry. The only place it might matter is the Planck scale, where quantum mechanics and gravity must meet — and that theory doesn't exist yet. So the GRAVITY lens is honest: present, and doing nothing here. And the scale stays a schematic — the real nucleus is ~1/100,000 of the atom, smaller than a pixel.
